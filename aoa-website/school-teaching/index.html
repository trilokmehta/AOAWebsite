The Reality Check: Why Schools are Different

Teaching Abacus in a private center is one thing; teaching it in a formal school environment is an entirely different challenge. In centers, students arrive fresh. In schools, they operate on a rigid, high-pressure timeline.

The "Assembly Line" Problem:

Students face back-to-back classes. They might move from History to Science, and then immediately to your Abacus class. By then, their cognitive fatigue is high.

Unlike open-ended tuition sessions, schools operate on strict bell timings (35 to 40 mins). You don't have the luxury of time; you need impact, and you need it fast.
