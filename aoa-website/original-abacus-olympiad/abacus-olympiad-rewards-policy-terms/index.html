Stage 2: The Knock-Out

Champion of Champions

Qualified meritorious students are given the opportunity to prepare for the Championship Round, after Stage 1 results. This is the elite league.

1. The "Precision" Route

Rank 1 Qualifiers
  • Goal: 100% Accuracy.
  • Rank 1 participants (100/100 score) get automatic entry regardless of time taken.
  • Tip: Encourage your child to maintain a steady pace with zero errors. One mistake drops them to Rank 2, where speed becomes a risk factor.

2. The "Agility" Route

Rank 2 & 3 Qualifiers
  • Goal: Top 30% Speed.
  • If scoring 95%-99%, the student enters the "Smart Kid Zone." Only the fastest 30% of students in these ranks qualify.
  • Tip: If your child usually makes 1-2 small mistakes, they must significantly increase writing/typing speed. Being accurate but slow here results in elimination.

The Final Challenge Format

Qualified participants enter a high-intensity championship featuring 15 Rounds of Speed & Accuracy. (Scoring includes Accuracy Marks + Speed Bonus - Negative Marking for errors)

  • Rank 1, 2, & 3: Champion of Champions (Per Category)
  • Medalists: Gold, Silver, and Bronze
  • Grand Honor: Awardees of the Knock-Out Round will receive a prestigious invitation to appear on stage at the next Annual Abacus Olympiad.

Participation Framework

Category Selection

All Students are encouraged to participate in both categories of examination, however Category A is MANDATORY.

  • Category A (MANDATORY): With Abacus. Candidate can appear in their current completed level.
  • Category M (Mental Math): Without Abacus. Candidate can appear in their current completed level, and up to one level below.

Exam Logistics

Day of the Exam
  • Duration: 45 minutes for Category A and 45 minutes for Category M.
  • Reporting Time: Report at least 30 minutes before the start.
  • Late Arrivals: No relaxation in time will be given.
  • Equipment: Students must bring their own Abacus.
  • Verification: Moderators will match faces with uploaded photographs.

Levels & Prep

Before the Exam
  • Selection: Instructors will sit with each student to decide the category.
  • Resources: Each student gets a minimum of 15 sample papers to rehearse.
  • Schedule: Date, Time, and Venue will be conveyed well in advance.

Authority & Prizes

Administration
  • Final Decision: Moderator decision is final regarding competition matters.
  • Association: Abacus Olympic Association decision is final for Prize distribution.
  • Ceremony: An exclusive Prize Distribution Ceremony will be held in New Delhi for visiting candidates (invitation to follow).
